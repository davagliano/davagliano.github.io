Method development for simulations in complex environment

We deveolp modern computational strategies to simulate chemistry and biology in complex environments. A particular effort is focus on the simulation of photochemistry happens in solvent and biological environments via QM/MM approaches and enhanced sampling techniques. We develop our own tools and contribute to open source simulation packages.

Photochemical induced protein and DNA binding

An applicative target of our research is the simulation of the mechanims of action of small organic ligand and fluorescent probes working as protein and DNA binders. We focus on characterization and design of new potential ligand and targets.

Machine learning for chemical and biological tasks

We develop machine learning techniques as a support of traditional computational methods. We employed artificial neural network and other architectures to solve more efficiently the scientific problems we face.

research from the user perspective

A milestone in our research is the development of user friendly, open source tools that are designed and developed from the user perspective. Our goal is to provide efficient and useful code and application that might speed up scientific discovery without the need of reprogrimming from scratch.
